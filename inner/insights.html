How it works

Capacitative
Sensing

Detecting and measuring the human dielectric

Cue science mumbo jumbo: Capacitors store charge based on an electrical difference between two separated, conductive plates. We can measure the capacitance of a capacitor by seeing how long it takes to charge with some current. When a human touches one of the plates, though, the capacitance of our circuit's capacitor changes. This occurs because humans, by virtue of being mostly water, are fairly conductive, and thus add a virtual ground to the capacitor plate being touched. By measuring this change in time to charge this new capacitor, we sense human touch.

Talking to Control Central

The capacitative sensor on the top of the chair transmits a signal to the Tush sensor, and wirelessly transmits information to a Dashboard that lets you know if there's a butt in the seat.
