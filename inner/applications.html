Applications

TUSH is a versatile sensor that can be put upon any surface that could ever come in contact with humans. It is material and form agnostic due to the flexibility in size and shape of the internal capacitative sensors. While it was inspired by seating in theaters and stadiums, field research shows that bars or restaurants, festivals, lectures, and any number of venues would benefit from the perfect economy of seats that tush allows.

Below is a small collection of what venues or systems we believe can be powered by Tush.

    Airplanes

    The Future of Travel

    While airport security and flight regulations make this use case a farther off application, we believe that there should be a better way of "gaming the system". It shouldn't be necessary to wait for hours on hold to try and get that last flight, and waiting for hours at the airport for standby shouldn't have to be reality. See seats get booked up as people sit in them, and climb aboard if there's one left. Get home this weekend.

    Restaurants

    The Future of Dining

    Oftentimes you are at the hands of the maitre'd and restaurant politics to try and get a seat at the bar. No more. You can now see what seats are available and snatch up that last minute reservation. Have your anniversary dinner at the place where you had your first date--even if you forgot to book in advance, because you know if there's anyone at the table.

    Stadiums

    Catch the ball game with your buddies.

    Stadium seating is finnicky. With the amount of seats available and the number of seats to choose from, it's nearly impossible to tell if there are actually seats open or if its just too crowded. Tush lets you know where there's an open seat, and allows you to grab those tickets -- even if the game is underway. Catch the game with pops, it's the home game!

    Lectures

    Attendance is a metric of success

    Tush lets you see exactly how many people are in attendance at a conference or lecture, and also enables analytics to allow you as the facilitator or the lecturer to know which events are popular, the crowd dynamics of your room, and more.

    Trains&Buses

    No seats for bags, no rush for a full train.

    Rush hour is stressful enough as it is without trying to run in heels to make your 5PM train home. Why run for a train that doesn't have a seat for you? Tush's capacitative sensing is smart enough to know if there's just a seat being taken up by someone's bag, or if there's a seat you can sit in.
